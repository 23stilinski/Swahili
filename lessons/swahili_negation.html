Kukanusha —
Negation in Swahili

How to say "I don't…", "She isn't…", "They won't…" across all persons and tenses.

The Negation Formula

NEG prefix + tense marker + verb stem
Positive: Ni + na + soma → Ninasoma (I am reading)
Negative: Si + — + soma → Sisomi (I am not reading)
⚠️ Key Rule In negation, the present tense -na- marker disappears and the verb often gains an -i ending.

Also: -ja- = "not yet" (perfect neg)
In Swahili, negation works by replacing the subject prefix with a special negative prefix. Think of it as swapping "Ni-" for "Si-", "A-" for "Ha-", etc. The rest of the sentence follows a pattern. Tense markers also change — some disappear, some become new negative-only markers!

1st Person

Mimi (I) Ni– Si–
Sisi (We) Tu– Hatu–
Ninakula → Sisomi
Tunakula → Hatukuli

2nd Person

Wewe (You) U– Hu–
Ninyi (You all) M– Ham–
Unasoma → Husomi
Mnasoma → Hamsomi

3rd Person

Yeye (He/She) A– Ha–
Wao (They) Wa– Hawa–
Anasoma → Hasomi
Wanasoma → Hawasomi

🔵 -ja- = "not yet" — The perfect negative uses -ja- instead of the positive -me-. This specifically means the action hasn't happened yet but might still happen.
Nimekula (I have eaten) → Sijala (I have not yet eaten)
🔴 -ku- in past negative — When negating the past tense, -ku- replaces -li-.
Nilikimbia (I ran) → Sikukimbia (I did not run)
🟢 Present drops -na- and adds -i — Present continuous negatives drop the -na- marker and the verb gains an -i ending.
Ninakula (I am eating) → Sikuli (I am not eating)

💬 See it in real conversation

Umekula leo asubuhi?Have you eaten this morning?
Sijala bado, nilikuwa na haraka.I haven't eaten yet, I was in a hurry.
Watoto hawakwenda shuleni?The children didn't go to school?
Hapana, hawatakwenda leo kwa sababu ya mvua.No, they will not go today because of rain.
Je, mnasoma Kiswahili?Are you all studying Swahili?
Ndiyo! Lakini hatusomi kila siku.Yes! But we don't study every day.
