🔑 The Secret Pattern — M- Words!

Notice how many body parts start with M-? That's Swahili's way of marking body nouns. Once you spot the pattern, it's easy!

M– →
Mkono Mguu Mdomo Mfupa Moyo Mgongo

👆 If you hear an M- word, there's a good chance it's a body part!

Memory Trick for the Face

MWILI — The Body

🧠

Memory Trick for the Body

Kifua (chest) is where your feelings live. Tumbo (stomach) — imagine a tummy drum going "tum-tum-bo!" Mgongo (back) — think "I'm gonna (mgonna) stretch my back!" Moyo (heart) sounds like "mayo" — your heart pumps like mayo squeezing out!

VIUNGO — Arms & Legs

🧠

Memory Trick for Limbs

Mkono (arm/hand) — "My cono" — imagine your arm holding an ice cream CONE! 🍦 Mguu (leg) — "M-goo" — your leg stepped in GOO! 🦶 Kidole (finger) — "Key-doll-ay" — your finger points at a KEY and a DOLL!

💪
Mkono
Arm / Hand
m-KOH-no
💡 In Swahili, Mkono means BOTH arm AND hand — one word for two things! Wave your arm: M-KOH-no! 👋

🎵 The Body Song!

Sing this to the tune of "Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes" — it really works!

Kichwa, mabega, magoti na vidole Head, shoulders, knees and fingers
Magoti na vidole! Knees and fingers!
Macho, masikio, mdomo na pua Eyes, ears, mouth and nose
Kichwa, mabega, magoti na vidole! Head, shoulders, knees and fingers!

💬 Use them in real sentences!

Kichwa changu kinauma. My head hurts. changu = my | kinauma = it hurts
Nina macho makubwa. I have big eyes. nina = I have | makubwa = big
Mkono wake ni mrefu. His/Her arm is long. wake = his/her | mrefu = long/tall
Tumbo langu lina njaa! My stomach is hungry! langu = my | njaa = hunger
Niosha mikono yako! Wash your hands! niosha = wash | yako = your | mikono = hands (plural of mkono)
Moyo wake unapiga. His/Her heart is beating. unapiga = it is beating/hitting
